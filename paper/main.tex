\begin{abstract}
Jamming attacks pose a critical threat to 5G networks, yet most existing detection methods rely on simulated data and lack deployment on standardized O-RAN architectures. We present MSJC, a Multi-Stage Jamming Classification xApp deployed on a real 5G O-RAN testbed that detects seven distinct jamming attack types---including protocol-aware attacks targeting PSS, PDCCH, and DMRS---with 100\% detection rate and 3.1\% false alarm rate. Our three-stage cascade combines: (1)~an MLP for fast 5-class KPI-based classification, (2)~a sliding-window KSVM that extracts 12-dimensional temporal statistics to detect intermittent attacks invisible to per-sample classifiers, and (3)~a MobileNetV3 for I/Q spectrogram-based protocol-aware classification. We further implement a closed-loop MLOps pipeline with GMM-based auto-labeling and automated retraining via the A1 interface. Experiments on the CCI~xG Testbed with three USRP~X310 software-defined radios demonstrate end-to-end inference latency $\leq$73\,ms, within the Near-RT~RIC 100\,ms budget.
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
5G, O-RAN, jamming detection, xApp, machine learning, Near-RT RIC, E2SM-KPM, closed-loop MLOps

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The Open Radio Access Network (O-RAN) architecture disaggregates the traditional RAN into programmable, multi-vendor components controlled by RAN Intelligent Controllers (RICs)~\cite{polese2023, abdalla2022}. While this openness enables innovation through xApps and rApps~\cite{santos2025, hoffmann2024}, it also expands the attack surface, making 5G networks increasingly vulnerable to sophisticated RF jamming~\cite{pirayesh2022, harvanek2024}.

Modern jamming attacks against 5G New Radio (NR) extend well beyond simple broadband noise. Protocol-aware jammers can selectively target synchronization signals (PSS/SSS), control channels (PDCCH), or reference signals (DMRS), each producing distinct spectral signatures but minimal impact on aggregate KPI metrics~\cite{savadatti2024, shahid2024}. Detecting and classifying these diverse attack types in real time is essential for effective mitigation.

Existing ML-based jamming detection approaches suffer from two key limitations. First, most rely on simulated data without validation on real 5G hardware~\cite{hachimi2020, kryszkiewicz2023}. Jere et al.~\cite{jere2024} demonstrated Bayesian-assisted ML on a 5G NR testbed but did not integrate with O-RAN interfaces. Second, static models cannot adapt to evolving attack patterns; Rahman et al.~\cite{rahman2025} proposed closed-loop retraining but limited to binary classification in simulation. Recent O-RAN-based approaches have begun addressing jamming on real testbeds~\cite{moore2025, bogucka2025, pazperez2025}, but none combine multi-class hierarchical classification with protocol-aware sub-typing and automated retraining in a single framework.

In this paper, we present MSJC (Multi-Stage Jamming Classification), a Near-RT RIC xApp validated on the CCI~xG Testbed~\cite{dasilva2025}. Our contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{3-Stage Classification Cascade:} Extending Hachimi et al.'s two-stage MLP$+$KSVM design~\cite{hachimi2020} from C-RAN to O-RAN, we add a third stage (MobileNetV3~\cite{howard2019}) for protocol-aware spectrogram classification, redesign the KSVM with sliding-window temporal features, expand from 4 to 7 jamming modes, and validate on real 5G hardware---achieving 100\% detection with $\leq$73\,ms latency.

    \item \textbf{Sliding-Window Statistical Features:} A 12-dimensional feature vector extracted from 15-second KPI windows that captures temporal patterns (BLER variance, periodicity, state transitions) invisible to per-sample classifiers, enabling detection of intermittent Random and Reactive jamming.

    \item \textbf{Closed-Loop MLOps on Real O-RAN:} Adopting Rahman et al.'s SAJD architecture~\cite{rahman2025}, we implement GMM-based Labeler rApp and Training Manager rApp and extend them from binary detection to multi-class classification, with end-to-end validation on a real 5G testbed---the first such demonstration on actual hardware.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Jamming Attacks and Detection}

Pirayesh and Zeng~\cite{pirayesh2022} provide a comprehensive survey of jamming attacks and anti-jamming strategies across wireless networks, covering constant, reactive, and deceptive jamming types. Savadatti et al.~\cite{savadatti2024} propose a six-dimensional taxonomy specific to 5G jamming, while Shahid et al.~\cite{shahid2024} present a protocol-agnostic classification of physical-layer jamming techniques. Harvanek et al.~\cite{harvanek2024} survey 5G PHY-layer security threats including jamming, spoofing, and eavesdropping, noting the growing role of ML-based countermeasures.

\subsection{ML-Based Jamming Classification}

Hachimi et al.~\cite{hachimi2020} proposed a two-stage ML-based intrusion detection system for 5G Cloud-RAN (C-RAN), combining an MLP for 4-class jamming classification (constant, deceptive, random, reactive) with a Kernelized SVM (KSVM) as a second stage to reduce false negatives. Their system achieved 94.51\% accuracy on the WSN-DS dataset but was limited to: (i)~simulated data without real RF validation, (ii)~C-RAN architecture without O-RAN integration, and (iii)~per-sample classification that cannot capture temporal attack patterns. Our work extends Hachimi's two-stage MLP$+$KSVM concept to a three-stage cascade by adding MobileNetV3~\cite{howard2019} for protocol-aware spectrogram classification (PSS, PDCCH, DMRS), redesigning the KSVM with sliding-window temporal statistics, expanding to seven jamming modes, and deploying on a real 5G O-RAN testbed with E2SM-KPM telemetry. Jere et al.~\cite{jere2024} demonstrated Bayesian inference-assisted ML for near-real-time jamming detection on a 5G NR testbed, achieving AUC $>$0.988 with 180\,ms detection delay. However, their work does not integrate with O-RAN interfaces or support protocol-aware sub-classification.

\subsection{O-RAN-Based Security xApps}

The O-RAN architecture~\cite{polese2023} enables deploying security functions as xApps on the Near-RT RIC. Santos et al.~\cite{santos2025} and Hoffmann et al.~\cite{hoffmann2024} provide comprehensive guides to xApp development, documenting practical challenges. Kryszkiewicz and Hoffmann~\cite{kryszkiewicz2023} proposed statistical jamming detection via O-RAN using 2D~Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests, but only in simulation. Moore et al.~\cite{moore2025} demonstrated LSTM-based intrusion detection with secure slicing xApps on a real testbed. Bogucka et al.~\cite{bogucka2025} implemented jamming detection and MCS adaptation on an Amarisoft-based O-RAN testbed. Paz-P\'{e}rez et al.~\cite{pazperez2025} designed a four-xApp cascade (monitoring, detection, classification, mitigation) on FlexRIC. Reus-Muns et al.~\cite{reusmuns2024} demonstrated spectrogram-based radar detection using a YOLO xApp, validating I/Q spectrogram ML on O-RAN infrastructure. Dimou and Noubir~\cite{dimou2025} proposed ARGOS for rogue base station detection using VAE-based anomaly detection on the same srsRAN+FlexRIC stack used in our work.

\subsection{Closed-Loop MLOps for O-RAN}

Stephenson et al.~\cite{stephenson2023} demonstrated closed-loop AI-driven RAN control on an SDR testbed at MILCOM~2023. Rahman et al.~\cite{rahman2025} proposed SAJD, a self-adaptive jamming \textit{detection} framework for O-RAN featuring a GMM-based Labeler rApp for unsupervised binary labeling, a Training Manager rApp with ClearML-driven retraining, and A1-interface model deployment. However, SAJD is limited to binary detection (jamming present/absent) without multi-class classification, and was validated only in simulation. Our work adopts Rahman's closed-loop MLOps architecture (Labeler rApp, Training Manager, A1 interface) and extends it from binary detection to hierarchical 7-class classification with protocol-aware sub-typing, all validated on a real testbed. Marinova and Leon-Garcia~\cite{marinova2024} survey intelligent O-RAN architectures, highlighting AI/ML workflow automation as a key research direction. Kakani et al.~\cite{kakani2025} address the complementary problem of adversarial attacks against xApp ML models, while Rumesh et al.~\cite{rumesh2024} explore federated learning for anomaly detection in O-RAN digital twins.

\section{System Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

\subsection{CCI xG Testbed}

All experiments are conducted on the CCI~xG Testbed~\cite{dasilva2025}, an O-RAN-compliant research platform at Virginia Tech. As shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:detection}, our setup uses three instances:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Instance-1 (UE):} srsUE running 5G NR SA on Band~3 FDD (1842.5\,MHz DL, 20\,MHz BW, 15\,kHz SCS, 106~PRBs).
    \item \textbf{Instance-2 (gNB + RIC):} srsRAN Project gNB with E2SM-KPM support, Open5GS 5G Core, and FlexRIC Near-RT RIC hosting the MSJC xApp.
    \item \textbf{Instance-3 (Jammer):} A custom 7-mode jammer generating protocol-aware RF interference, with OFDM structure matched to the gNB (FFT=1536, CP=108).
\end{itemize}

Each instance is equipped with an Ettus USRP X310 SDR connected via 10G Ethernet (MTU~9000). The gNB reports per-UE KPI metrics via E2SM-KPM at 1-second intervals.

\subsection{MSJC Classification Pipeline}

The xApp implements a three-stage cascade optimized for the Near-RT RIC's 100\,ms latency budget:

\textbf{Stage~1 (MLP):} Classifies each 8-dimensional KPI vector into five classes (Normal, Constant, Random, Reactive, Deceptive) in 1--2\,ms. Constant, Random, and Reactive detections are confirmed immediately.

\textbf{Stage~2 (KSVM):} Re-examines Normal verdicts using a sliding window of $W=15$ samples (15~seconds). Twelve statistical features capture temporal patterns invisible to per-sample analysis. An RBF-kernel SVM decides Normal vs.\ Attack in 3--4\,ms.

\textbf{Stage~3 (MobileNetV3):} Invoked only for Deceptive detections or Stage~2 false-negative catches. Raw I/Q snapshots (16,384 complex samples via passive USRP reception) are converted to 224$\times$224 spectrograms~\cite{reusmuns2024} and classified into PSS/SSS, PDCCH, DMRS, or Generic Deceptive in 40--50\,ms.

\subsection{Closed-Loop MLOps}

Two Non-RT RIC rApps automate the ML lifecycle, adopting the SAJD closed-loop architecture proposed by Rahman et al.~\cite{rahman2025} and extending it from binary detection to multi-class classification:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Labeler rApp:} Implements Rahman's GMM-based unsupervised labeling pipeline (moving average smoothing $\rightarrow$ ARC drift detection with $\tau = 0.0004$ $\rightarrow$ 2-component GMM clustering) to generate binary Normal/Attack annotations from raw KPI telemetry.
    \item \textbf{Training Manager rApp:} Monitors xApp accuracy via InfluxDB, triggers Stage~1 MLP retraining when accuracy drops below 30\%, and deploys updated models via an A1-like HTTP interface---extending Rahman's ClearML-based orchestration with a standalone fallback mode.
\end{itemize}

\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

\subsection{KPI Feature Extraction}

Each E2SM-KPM report is mapped to an 8-dimensional feature vector: RSRP (PUSCH SNR proxy), RSRQ, SINR (PUCCH), BLER, UCI NACK rate, DL throughput, CQI, and HARQ retransmission rate. In practice, SINR, BLER, and CQI are the primary discriminators due to the srsRAN DU KPM provider's metric scope.

\subsection{Stage 1: MLP 5-Class Classifier}

Following Hachimi et al.'s two-stage design~\cite{hachimi2020}, our Stage~1 uses an MLP as the first-pass classifier. We extend the original 4-class scheme to 5~classes (adding Deceptive) and replace raw I/Q features with 8-dimensional E2SM-KPM metrics. The architecture (8$\rightarrow$256$\rightarrow$128$\rightarrow$64$\rightarrow$5) with BatchNorm, ReLU, and Dropout~(0.3) totals 44,677 parameters. Training uses synthetic KPI profiles calibrated to real testbed measurements, with clean-looking attack samples filtered to prevent Normal/Deceptive overlap.

\subsection{Stage 2: Sliding-Window KSVM}

We extract 12 statistical features from $W=15$ consecutive KPI reports:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{f} = [\mu_b, \sigma^2_b, b_{\max}, r_b, \mu_s, \sigma^2_s, s_{\min}, \sigma^2_c, c_{\min}, \mu_n, \rho_b, \tau_b]
\end{equation}

\noindent where $b$, $s$, $c$, $n$ denote BLER, SINR, CQI, NACK; $\rho_b$ is BLER autocorrelation (periodicity); $\tau_b$ counts BLER state transitions. While Hachimi et al.~\cite{hachimi2020} used a per-sample KSVM as a binary false-negative recheck, our sliding-window approach captures temporal dynamics---BLER variance, periodicity, and state transitions---that are invisible to single-sample analysis. The KSVM (RBF, $C$=10) is trained directly on real testbed windows rather than simulated data.

\subsection{Stage 3: MobileNetV3 Protocol Classification}

MobileNetV3-Small~\cite{howard2019} (1.52M parameters) classifies 224$\times$224 spectrograms into four protocol-aware sub-types. Initial training on synthetic spectrograms is followed by fine-tuning with 200 real I/Q captures to bridge the sim-to-real domain gap, as motivated by the spectrogram-based approach demonstrated in SenseORAN~\cite{reusmuns2024}.

\subsection{Closed-Loop Demonstration}

We validated the complete cycle: xApp processes E2SM-KPM telemetry and logs to InfluxDB $\rightarrow$ Labeler rApp generates GMM labels $\rightarrow$ Training Manager retrains Stage~1 MLP and sends MODEL\_UPDATE via A1 $\rightarrow$ xApp hot-reloads updated weights without service interruption. The full cycle completes within 5~minutes.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented MSJC, a multi-stage jamming classification xApp for 5G O-RAN achieving 100\% detection across seven jamming modes with 3.1\% false alarm rate and $\leq$73\,ms latency on the CCI~xG Testbed. Key innovations include sliding-window temporal features for intermittent attack detection, real I/Q fine-tuning for protocol-aware classification, and a closed-loop MLOps pipeline with A1-interface deployment. Future work includes multi-cell extension, E2SM-RC active mitigation~\cite{abdalla2022}, sub-second KPM reporting, and adversarial robustness~\cite{kakani2025}.
